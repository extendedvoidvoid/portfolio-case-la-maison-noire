## Literary / dramatic source of the mise en scène

Two related “waiting woman on the phone” texts are repeatedly cited:

1. **Dorothy Parker — *A Telephone Call*** (woman alone, obsessive wait for a call that may never come / may be imagined; self-interrogation, rising panic).  
2. Homage notes also link to **Jean Cocteau — *La Voix humaine*** (woman alone with the telephone as sole partner).

**Consequence for mise en scène:** the set is built as a **psychological chamber** — one woman, one space, one object of obsession (the phone) — not as a multi-location pop clip.

---

## Découpage de la mise en scène (what the camera / set *do*)

### 1. Espace (space)
- **Un seul décor principal:** apartment / hotel-room / boudoir feel — “civil rights–era hotel room” (Slant) or apartment + bed (Wikipedia).  
- **Closed world:** outside almost doesn’t matter; the room *is* the drama.  
- **Studio reconstruction** (Silvercup) → controlled light, not documentary street.

### 2. Temps (time)
- **Duration as action:** she fills the entire song length with waiting.  
- **Repetition / subtle loop energy** (commentators: “extremely subtle and repetitive”) — same space, escalating micro-gestures.  
- **Slow** black-and-white rhythm = mini-movie, not cut-heavy 90s dance video.

### 3. Personnage dans le cadre (figure in frame)
- Alone — no lover on screen.  
- **Wordless tactile performance:**  
  - paces  
  - wrings hands  
  - removes false eyelashes  
  - listlessly tidies  
  - rummages dresser / tries on clothes to kill time  
- Escalating **private desperation** under controlled surface (Slant).  
- Ambiguity: is the “fix” romantic — or something else? (open reading).

### 4. Objet dramatique (prop that carries plot)
- **The telephone** = sole dramatic partner (Parker / Cocteau logic).  
- She longs but “does not dare” to call (Wikipedia summary).  
- **Climax of mise en scène:** the call finally comes → **she hangs up** (refusal / self-command).

### 5. Lumière & matière (light & material)
- **Black and white** — high contrast “noir” / mini-film grain (viewer comments: piece of art, raw).  
- Interior lamp / soft hard hybrid; intimate, not glamour key only.  
- Texture of room, bed, clothes, hands — **tactile** more than spectacular.

### 6. Caméra / rythme (camera / rhythm)
- Slow; holds; “mini movie” construction.  
- Focus on **face + hands + object**, not choreography spectacle.  
- Ending is an **act of framing power**: she cuts the connection (hang up) rather than being claimed by the caller.

### 7. Son (for context)
- Trip-hop / Massive Attack production + cinematic strings (Craig Armstrong often cited in song discourse) → audio space is dark, spacious, sensual; image stays sparse.

## Schéma narratif de la mise en scène

```
ROOM (closed)
  → WAIT (time stretched)
    → GESTURES (fill anxiety: body, clothes, hands, phone)
      → CALL (object answers)
        → HANG UP (she refuses the thing she wanted)
```

No product sell. No multi-set travelogue. **One room, one woman, one object, one refusal.**

## What this gives *our* fragrance film (translation, not copy)

| *I Want You* mise en scène | Our *Tu me connais* translation |
|----------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Closed room | One quiet interior + window (not palace) |
| Waiting as plot | Duration / almost-known / frame watches |
| Telephone as obsession object | **Discreet blank flacon** (not giant bottle, **no logo**) |
| Trying clothes / hands / lashes | Fabric beat, spray gesture, restless hands |
| Hang up | Empty room + **black** (refusal of the “name” / the claim of the gaze) |
| B&W slow mini-film | Keep |
| Parker inner monologue | No VO — gesture only |

**Do not copy:** period phone as hero prop, Madonna likeness, literal Cocteau set-dressing.
