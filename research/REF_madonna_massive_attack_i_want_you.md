## Narration (what the video *tells*, almost without plot speech)

The song’s lyrics are desire (*I want you / want you to want me*).  
The **video’s story** is different and quieter:

1. **A woman alone in a room** — disheveled, waiting.  
2. **Time is the enemy** — she paces, wrings hands, removes false lashes, tidies, tries on clothes to fill the wait.  
3. **The telephone** is the object of obsession (not a lover on screen).  
4. **Ambiguity** — is the lover real? is the fix romantic or chemical? (Slant: hints of more than a lover.)  
5. **Ending power** — when the call finally comes, **she hangs up**. She refuses the thing she waited for.

So narration = **inner monologue of waiting made physical**, not product explanation, not romance resolution.

Dorothy Parker energy: anxiety, repetition, self-interrogation — *will he call?* — rendered as **gesture and duration**, not VO paragraphs.
